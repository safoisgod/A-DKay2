Our Story

From a vision to amplify African voices to a thriving publishing house, here’s how we began.

2020: The Beginning

A-D Kay Publications was founded by Kojo Ampem Daako.

A-D Kay Publications was founded by Kojo Ampem Daako with a mission to amplify African voices through diverse genres like politics, tradition, and culture. His vision was to create a platform where African stories could reach a global audience.

2021: First Book Release

We published our first book, marking a significant milestone.

In 2021, A-D Kay Publications released its first book, a collection of essays on African politics by Kojo Ampem Daako. This debut set the tone for our commitment to thought-provoking literature.

2022: Key Partnerships

We partnered with global platforms to expand our reach.

In 2022, we formed partnerships with platforms like Amazon, Kobo, and Apple Books, making our titles accessible to readers worldwide and significantly boosting our visibility.

2023: Transition & Growth

After Kojo’s passing, Nana Kwesi Safo took over as CEO.

After Kojo’s passing in 2023, his son Nana Kwesi Safo took over as CEO, continuing to nurture talent and expand our global reach while honoring his father’s legacy.

2025: Today

Five years strong, redefining local literature.

Now 5 years strong, we’re redefining local literature with 10+ titles, 1,600+ readers, and a commitment to global impact through diverse storytelling.

Our Mission & Vision

We’re here to preserve heritage, spark meaningful discourse, and create a lasting impact.

Our Mission

At A-D Kay Publications, our mission is to amplify African voices by publishing stories that resonate with youth, Africans, educators, and businesses worldwide. We believe in the transformative power of storytelling—whether it’s through fiction, non-fiction, educational books, or romantic poems—to preserve heritage, address social issues, and foster global connections.

Our Vision

Our vision is to redefine local literature by embracing diverse genres and innovative publishing practices, ensuring that African narratives not only endure but also inspire the next generation of readers and thinkers.
